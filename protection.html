How to Protect Yourself Online

  • Use strong, unique passwords (12+ characters, mix of letters, numbers, and symbols). Use a password manager to never repeat passwords.
  • Turn on Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) wherever possible. It adds a second layer of protection.
  • Update your software, devices, and apps regularly. Enable auto-updates to stay safe from new threats.
  • Stay alert for phishing scams. Don’t click suspicious links or open attachments from unknown senders.
  • Back up your important files to a secure cloud or external drive. This helps if you get ransomware or lose a device.
  • Limit app permissions on your phone and computer. Only allow access that's absolutely required.
  • Lock your devices with a PIN, fingerprint, or face ID. Don’t leave them unattended.
