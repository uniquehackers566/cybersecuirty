Types of Cyber Attacks

1. Phishing

Fake emails or messages that trick users into giving up sensitive data like passwords, credit card details, or clicking malicious links.

2. Malware

Short for "malicious software": viruses, worms, Trojans, and spyware that steal or destroy information.

3. Ransomware

Blocks access to files or systems until a ransom is paid. Examples include WannaCry and REvil.

4. DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service)

Overwhelms a system with traffic from multiple sources, making it unusable for real users.

5. SQL Injection

Attackers insert malicious SQL code into forms or URLs to access or delete data from the backend database.

6. Man-in-the-Middle (MitM)

Attackers secretly intercept communication between two parties to eavesdrop or alter data.

7. Zero-Day Exploits

Attacks that exploit unknown or unpatched vulnerabilities before developers can fix them.

8. Insider Threats

Malicious or careless employees who leak, sell, or destroy sensitive internal data.
